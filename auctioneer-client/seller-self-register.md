# How does a seller self-register as a customer from the invitation email? What is the seller’s role after becoming a customer of the auctioneer?

This guide is for **you** if an **auction company** invited you by email to register on Auction Journal. You are **not** signing up as an auctioneer and you do **not** use the Auctioneer Dashboard. You complete a short **onboarding form** on the web; that creates your profile as a **customer (client)** of that auctioneer.

**Auctioneer staff:** see [How can an auctioneer invite a seller to self-register?](invite-seller.md).

---

## What the invitation is for

The email says you were invited by an auctioneer (their name and company) to register as a **seller** on Auction Journal. In practice that means:

- You become a **customer** of **that auction company** in their system.  
- Your main relationship from this invite is as a **consigner (seller)**—someone who can **sell property through their auctions**.  
- The auctioneer runs the sale; you provide items and work with them on listings, clerking, and **settlement** for your consignments.  
- You do **not** get login access to run auctions yourself unless you separately register as an auctioneer on Auction Journal.

Select **Register as Seller** (or the registration button) in the email to open the form.

---

## Before you start

- Use the link **within 24 hours** of receiving it. After that, the link expires and you must ask the auctioneer to **send a new invite**.  
- The link only works if it includes all required details. If you see **Invalid Request**, contact the auctioneer to resend the invitation.  
- Have ready: driver’s licence number (required), phone number (**+1** and 10 digits), and billing (and optional shipping) address.  
- Optional: company name, second phone, date of birth, notes, profile photo, ID card images, and “how did you find us” text.

---

## Step 1 — Open the link from your email

1. Open the message from Auction Journal (subject like **Invitation to Join [Company Name] on Auction Journal**).  
2. Select **Register as Seller**.  
3. Your browser opens the **Onboarding User!** page (Auction Journal logo and a 2-step progress bar).

If the page says **Invalid Request — Please contact your auctioneer to resend invitation request**, the link is incomplete or expired. Do not continue; contact the auction company.

---

## Step 2 — Add your information (step 1 of 2)

On **Add Your Information**, fill in the required fields (marked with **\***):

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Driver licence number | Yes | You can use the **scanner** icon to scan a licence and pre-fill some fields |
| First name, Last name | Yes | |
| Company name | No | |
| Phone 1 | Yes | Must be **+1** followed by 10 digits (e.g. +15551234567) |
| Phone 2 | No | If used, must start with **+1** |
| E-mail | Yes | **Cannot be changed**—it matches the address the auctioneer invited |
| DOB | No | |
| Notes | No | |
| Client image | No | Photo upload; max 8 MB, common image types |
| How did client find about you? | No | |
| ID card | No | State ID or Driving licence; optional front/back uploads |

Select **Save & Next** when step 1 is complete. Fix any red error messages before continuing.

---

## Step 3 — Addresses (step 2 of 2)

**Bill to** (billing address):

- **Address 1**, **Zip**, **City**, **State**, and **Country** are required.  
- **Address 2** is optional.  
- Entering a US **zip** may auto-fill city, state, and country.

**Ship to**:

- Check **Shipping address same as billing address** if both are identical.  
- Otherwise complete **Ship to** the same way.

Select **Previous** to go back and edit step 1, or **Save** to submit.

---

## Step 4 — Confirmation

After a successful submit you see **You are All Set!** — your information was sent to the auctioneer’s Auction Journal account.

You can close the page. You do **not** need to create a separate Auction Journal bidder account for this step unless you also plan to bid online at their auctions (that is a different registration on the public site).

---

## What is your role after you register?

You are now a **customer (client)** of that **one auctioneer**. In Auction Journal terms:

| Role | What it means for you |
|------|------------------------|
| **Consigner (seller)** | The invite and email describe you as a **seller**. You are the person or company **consigning property** for that auctioneer to sell. They manage the auction; you track your lots and settlements with them through their process (they use your profile in their dashboard). |
| **Buyer** | This invite flow does **not** ask you to set up buyer settings. If you also **purchase** at their auctions, the auctioneer can configure buyer options on your profile later, or you may register as a **bidder** on Auction Journal when signing up for a sale. |
| **Not an auctioneer** | You cannot create auctions or access the Auctioneer Dashboard from this onboarding. |

**What you should expect:**

- The auction company sees you under **Customers** in their dashboard.  
- They may contact you to consign items, agree commissions, and run settlements after sales.  
- For changes to your email or a new link, contact **that auctioneer**, not Auction Journal general support, unless you have a platform account issue.

**What you should do if something goes wrong:**

| Problem | What to do |
|---------|------------|
| Link expired | Ask the auctioneer to send a new **Onboard Seller** invite |
| “Email already exist as your client” when they invite again | You may already be registered—ask them to look you up in **Customers** |
| Wrong email on the form | Ask the auctioneer to invite the correct address |
| Error on submit | Note the message, fix required fields, or contact the auctioneer |
