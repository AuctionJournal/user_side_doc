# Who is a floor bidder? How do I onboard one?

A **floor bidder** is a **customer (client)** of **your** auction company who takes part in a sale **in person**—on the auction floor—not by logging into the public Auction Journal bidder website.

Floor bidders are common on **Onsite With Live Webcast** auctions: the sale runs online for remote bidders, while people in the room bid through you. **You** enter their bids in the system during live bidding, because they can bid directly in the room.

---

## Who is a floor bidder?

| | **Floor bidder** | **Online bidder** |
|---|------------------|-------------------|
| **Account** | Your **client** in **Customers** | Auction Journal **bidder** login on the public site |
| **How they bid** | You record bids for them (live webcast / clerking) | They bid from their own device |
| **Typical auction** | **Onsite With Live Webcast** | Any auction they register for online |
| **In your client list** | Shown as **Floor Bidder** | Shown as **Normal** (if also a linked client) |

A floor bidder is still a **buyer** for your business: they get a **bid card number**, can appear on registration lists, and are included in clerking and settlement like other buyers.

They are **not** the same as someone who already has a full **bidder account** on Auction Journal. If that person should bid online, they should use their bidder login—not be set up as a floor-only client with the same email.

---

## What you do in two steps

Onboarding has **two parts**:

1. **Create the person as a floor bidder client** (your customer database).  
2. **Add them to a specific auction** (check-in for that sale).

You need both before they can be used for bidding on that auction.

---

## Step 1 — Onboard as a customer (floor bidder client)

1. Sign in to the **Auctioneer Dashboard**.  
2. Open **Customers**.  
3. Select **Onboard Floor Bidder**.  
4. Complete the wizard (**Register Floor Bidder**):
   - **Clients info** — client code (generate if needed), name, phone, **email**, licence, and related fields.  
   - **Address** — billing (and shipping if needed).  
   - **Accounting** — tax and buyer’s premium settings for this buyer.  
   - **Additional info** — any extra fields your workflow uses.  
5. Submit.

The new record appears in **Customers** with type **Floor Bidder**.

**Important:** Do not use this flow for someone who **already** has an Auction Journal **bidder** account with the same email. The system will block it. They should register for your auction from their bidder account instead.

---

## Step 2 — Add the floor bidder to an auction

Full walkthrough (wizard, screenshots, three paths): **[How do I check in a floor bidder for an auction?](../../auction/floor-bidder-check-in.md)**.

Summary: **Auction Dashboard** → **Registration** → **Check-in Floor Bidder** (onsite live webcast, within registration dates). Choose whether they are already an Auction Journal bidder, whether they will sign up as one, or register an existing **floor bidder** client with **Register**.

---

## During the auction

- On **live webcast**, you choose the floor bidder and **enter bids** on their behalf as bids come in from the floor.  
- In **clerking**, lots can be awarded to the floor bidder when appropriate.  
- The **Registration** area lists floor bidders separately from online registrants where the product shows that split.

Remote bidders still bid online; floor bidders are handled through your staff actions in the dashboard.

---

## When to use a floor bidder vs an online bidder

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Person is in the room, no AJ login | **Onboard Floor Bidder** + **Check-in** on the auction |
| Person already uses Auction Journal as a bidder | They should **register for the auction** online; they become your client automatically |
| Person will sign up on AJ later | Check-in flow can **invite** by email (optional path in **Check-in Floor Bidder**) |
