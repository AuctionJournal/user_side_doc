# Why was my registration for an auction rejected or still pending?

After you submit **Auction Registration** on a sale, you get **approved**, **pending**, or **declined**. This page explains **why** each outcome happens and **what you can do**.

For how to register in the first place, see [How do I register for an auction?](register-for-auction.md).

---

## The three outcomes (after you submit)

| Outcome | Can you bid on this auction? | What it usually means |
|---------|------------------------------|------------------------|
| **Approved** | Yes, when bidding is open for that sale | You are cleared up to the **bid limit** shown (maximum bid amount for this auction). |
| **Pending** | **No** — not until the auctioneer approves you | Your registration is **on hold** for manual review. |
| **Declined** | **No** | You cannot bid this auction. You may need to talk to the auctioneer about **bid permission**. |

You receive an **email** when registration completes or when the auctioneer later changes your status.

---

## Why registration is still pending

**Pending** means the auctioneer must **approve or decline** you before you can bid. The platform does **not** auto-approve pending registrations later on its own.

Common reasons you land in **pending**:

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Bidder score below zero** | The auctioneer uses **default** bid permission on your customer record, and your Auction Journal **bidder score** is negative at registration time. Low trust history triggers review. See [What is bidder score?](../bidder-score/score.md). |
| **Auctioneer policy** | Some companies review every new registrant even when score is fine—especially for high-value sales. |

### What to do if you are pending

1. Read the on-screen message (it usually asks you to **contact the auctioneer**).
2. Email or call the **auction company** running the sale—not Auction Journal support for bid approval.
3. Wait for **approval** or a clear **decline**; you cannot submit the registration form again for the same auction.
4. Check your email for an update when they change your status.

---

## Why registration was declined

**Declined** means you are **not allowed to bid** that auction. Contact the auctioneer if you believe it is a mistake.

Common reasons:

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Decline all bids** on your customer record | The auctioneer set your profile to **decline all bids** (with a reason). That applies across their auctions, including this registration. |
| **Manual decline** | They reviewed your registration (or a prior relationship) and declined you for **this sale**. |
| **Score or history** | Combined with their rules—similar to pending, but they (or policy) chose **decline** instead of waiting. |

Messages often mention **bid permission** because the auctioneer controls caps and approval on their **customer** list. They can change standing for **future** auctions separately from fixing one sale.

### What to do if you are declined

1. Contact the **auctioneer** for that sale and ask whether they can change bid permission or registration status.
2. Do **not** expect to re-register online for the same auction—you already have one registration row.
3. Improve **bidder score** over time ([how to improve score](../bidder-score/score.md)) if low score contributed to their decision.

---

## Why you might not be approved even when you expected it

These checks happen **before** or **during** registration; they are different from pending/declined:

| Issue | What happens |
|-------|----------------|
| **Not signed in** | You must log in as a bidder first. |
| **Registration window closed** | Registration is only between the auction **start** and **end** dates on the listing. |
| **ID not on file** | Upload driving licence / state ID when prompted. |
| **Verified bidder required** | The auction needs a **verified** bidder (ID + card). Complete [verification](verification.md). |
| **No card selected** | Verified-bidder auctions require choosing or adding a card on the registration form. |
| **Terms not accepted** | You must check the terms box before **Accept**. |

---

## Approved with a bid limit

Even when **approved**, you only bid up to the **maximum bid amount** on your registration (from the auction default, an **invite**, or the auctioneer’s customer rules). If your bids are rejected near a cap, the limit—not pending status—is the issue.

---

## One registration per auction

After you register once—**any** status—you **cannot** submit the registration form again for that auction. If your situation should change (pending → approved), only the **auctioneer** can update permission from their **Auction Dashboard**.
